Assistant: Maintaining Full-Time Status

To maintain your visa status as an international student, you need to be enrolled in at least 9 credit hours per semester, with at least 6 of those being in-person classes. Here's a structured plan to help you manage both academic and immigration requirements:

Current Courses
CSC 532 - Design & Analysis Of Algorithm I (3 credits, In Progress)
MIS 534 - Information Security Management (3 credits, In Progress)
CSC 550 - Software Engineering (3 credits, In Progress)
MIS 503 - Programming for Analytics (3 credits, In Progress)

You are currently taking 12 credit hours, which satisfies the full-time requirement. Ensure that at least two of these courses have in-person components.

Academic Requirements

Core Courses Needed:
1. Network Programming: CSC 544
2. Database Management Systems: MIS 555
3. Analysis, Modeling and Design: MIS 565

These core courses are essential for completing your degree requirements.

Research or Thesis Requirement:
You will need to choose between completing a research project or thesis.

Steps to Follow

1. Check Course Schedule: Make sure you register for the required in-person courses next semester.
2. Plan Ahead: Ensure that your future course selections align with your degree requirements while maintaining full-time status.
3. Consult Your Advisor: Regularly meet with your academic advisor to ensure you're on track for both graduation and maintaining visa compliance.
