Assistant: Certainly! Here's a concise comparison of the research project and thesis options, along with their key milestones:

Research Project

Overview:
Practical application of knowledge to solve real-world problems.
Typically less extensive than a thesis.

Key Milestones:
1. Topic Selection and Approval: 
Choose a topic with faculty guidance.
2. Written Analysis and Presentation:
Complete a written analysis and present your findings.
3. Defense:
Successfully defend your project (DEFN).

Completion Requirements:
Must complete the final project (PROJ).
Generally involves 6 credits.

Thesis

Overview:
Original research contributing to academic knowledge.
More comprehensive than a research project.

Key Milestones:
1. Comprehensive Oral Exam: 
Pass an oral exam covering your field (COMO).
2. Thesis Defense: 
Defend your thesis in front of a committee (DEFN).
3. Draft Submission: 
Submit an electronic draft by the deadline (DRAT).
4. Format Approval Sheet: 
Submit this sheet with your draft for format verification (FORM).
5. Final Submission: 
Submit the final version electronically by the deadline (THES).
6. Signed Title Page: 
Include a signed title page with the final submission (TITL).
7. ETD Submission Form: 
Complete and submit this form with your final thesis (ETDF).

Completion Requirements:
Involves intensive research, usually over multiple semesters.

Summary
The *research project* is more applied, focusing on practical solutions.
The *thesis* involves deeper, original research.
Both require defense presentations but differ in scope and depth.

Choosing between them depends on whether you prefer practical application or academic research, as well as your career goals.
