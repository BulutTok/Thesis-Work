Assistant: Core Courses Required for MScIS Program

Here are the core courses you need to complete for your Master of Science in Computer Science & Information Systems program:

1. CSC 532 - Design and Analysis of Algorithms I
Prerequisites: None specified.

2. MIS 534 - Information Security Management
Prerequisites: BUS 500 and CSC 344 or MIS 323.

3. CSC 544 - Network Programming
Prerequisites: CSC 500 and CSC 344 or MIS 323.

4. CSC 550 - Software Engineering
Prerequisites: CSC 500 and CSC 450 or MIS 411.

5. MIS 555 - Database Management Systems
Prerequisites: BUS 500 and CSC 455 or MIS 315.

6. MIS 565 - Analysis, Modeling, and Design
Prerequisites: BUS 500 and CSC 450 or MIS 411.

Actionable Steps
Ensure you meet the prerequisites before enrolling in each course.
Plan your course sequence to align with any prerequisite requirements.
Consider meeting with an academic advisor to discuss your plan.

Summary

You need to complete six core courses for your degree, each having specific prerequisites that must be met prior to enrollment. Make sure to verify your eligibility based on these requirements as you plan your coursework.
