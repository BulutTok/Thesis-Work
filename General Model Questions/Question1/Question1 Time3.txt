Assistant: Core Courses Required for the MSc in Computer Science & Information Systems

For your Master of Science in Computer Science & Information Systems program, you need to complete the following core courses:

1. CSC 532 - Design and Analysis of Algorithms I
Prerequisite: CSC 500 (Introduction to Computer Science)

2. MIS 534 - Information Security Management
Prerequisites: BUS 500 and CSC 344 or MIS 323

3. CSC 544 - Network Programming
Prerequisites: CSC 500 and CSC 344 or MIS 323

4. CSC 550 - Software Engineering
Prerequisites: CSC 500 and CSC 450 or MIS 411

5. MIS 555 - Database Management Systems
Prerequisites: BUS 500 and CSC 455 or MIS 315

6. MIS 565 - Analysis, Modeling and Design
Prerequisites: BUS 500 and CSC 450 or MIS 411

Actionable Steps
Review your academic transcript to ensure you have completed the necessary prerequisite courses.
If any prerequisites are not yet fulfilled, consider enrolling in them first to prepare for the core courses.
Schedule a meeting with your academic advisor if you need assistance planning your course load.

Conclusion
Completing these core courses is essential for fulfilling the requirements of your MScIS program. Make sure to address any prerequisite gaps early on to ensure a smooth progression through your studies. If further assistance is needed, don't hesitate to reach out!
