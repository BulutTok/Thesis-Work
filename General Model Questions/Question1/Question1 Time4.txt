Assistant: Core Courses Required for the MSc in Computer Science & Information Systems

For your Master of Science in Computer Science & Information Systems program, you are required to complete the following core courses:

1. CSC 532 - Design and Analysis of Algorithms I
Prerequisite: CSC 500 (Introduction to Computer Science)

2. MIS 534 - Information Security Management
Prerequisites: BUS 500 and CSC 344 or MIS 323

3. CSC 544 - Network Programming
Prerequisites: CSC 500 and CSC 344 or MIS 323

4. CSC 550 - Software Engineering
Prerequisites: CSC 500 and CSC 450 or MIS 411

5. MIS 555 - Database Management Systems
Prerequisites: BUS 500 and CSC 455 or MIS 315

6. MIS 565 - Analysis, Modeling and Design
Prerequisites: BUS 500 and CSC 450 or MIS 411

Actionable Steps
Review your academic history to ensure you have completed the necessary prerequisites for each core course.
If any prerequisites are missing, plan to enroll in those courses first.
Contact your academic advisor if you need assistance with planning your course schedule.

Conclusion
These core courses form the foundation of your MScIS program, so it's crucial to complete any prerequisite requirements beforehand. This will help ensure a smooth progression through your graduate studies. If you need further guidance on scheduling or fulfilling these requirements, feel free to reach out for additional support!
