Assistant: Core Courses Required for MScIS Program

Based on your degree audit, here are the core courses required for the Master of Science in Computer Science and Information Systems (MScIS) program at UNC Wilmington:

1. CSC 532 - Design and Analysis of Algorithms I
Prerequisites: CSC 500
Description: Theory of the design of efficient computer algorithms including sorting, searching, pattern matching, and more.

2. MIS 534 - Information Security Management
Prerequisites: BUS 500 and CSC 344 or MIS 323
Description: Examination of principles and processes of security management in networked systems.

3. CSC 544 - Network Programming
Prerequisites: CSC 500 and CSC 344 or MIS 323
Description: Implementation of network programming concepts using various programming languages on Unix or Windows platforms.

4. CSC 550 - Software Engineering
Prerequisites: CSC 500 and CSC 450 or MIS 411
Description: Introduction to software life cycle models with an emphasis on large development projects.

5. MIS 555 - Database Management Systems
Prerequisites: BUS 500 and CSC 455 or MIS 315
Description: Study of the design and administration of database systems in a business environment.

6. MIS 565 - Analysis, Modeling and Design
Prerequisites: BUS 500 and CSC 450 or MIS 411
Description: Topics include project estimation, management, logical design methodologies, risk analysis, implementation issues, etc.
